What is accessible tourism?

Tourism with social and economic benefits

It’s a recognition of the growing number of people with physical disabilities who travel. It sits under a broader idea of ‘Inclusive Tourism’ –  referring to the push to welcome visitors of all abilities and backgrounds.

For destinations, it means being mindful that more than 10% of your visitors will have reduced mobility. They might use a wheelchair or maybe they’re just not steady on their feet.

These people are part of a cohort who are travelling more often, they're travelling in groups and they can be very loyal customers.

About Mention Mobility.

Information is confidence.

Especially for people with reduced mobility (PRM) who travel.
